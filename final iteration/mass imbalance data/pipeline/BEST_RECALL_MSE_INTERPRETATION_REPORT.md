# Interpretation of Train/Test MSE for Best-Recall Models

This section explains two observations from the best-recall model results: first, why the training and testing MSE values are often close and why testing MSE is sometimes lower than training MSE; second, why Symbolic Regression (SR) and Hybrid SR can have lower MSE in some cases even though SINDy and Hybrid SINDy generally recover the equation structure better.

## Train/Test MSE Behavior

The training and testing MSE values are close in many cases because the data are synthetic and the train/test samples are drawn from the same underlying governing dynamics. Within each regime, the train and test data are not fundamentally different physical systems; they are different portions of trajectories generated by the same equations of motion. Therefore, when a discovered model captures the dominant regime dynamics, it can generalize to the held-out segment with a similar error level.

In several cases, the testing MSE is lower than the training MSE. This does not necessarily indicate data leakage or an invalid split. The best-recall models were selected according to structural recovery, not by minimizing training error. As a result, the selected model is not guaranteed to be the model with the smallest training MSE. A sparse or structurally correct model can fit the training segment less tightly while still predicting the test segment well, especially if the test segment is smoother or dynamically easier.

The results support this interpretation. For the best-recall models, test MSE is lower than train MSE in 7 of 9 SINDy cases, 8 of 9 Hybrid SINDy cases, 6 of 9 SR cases, and 8 of 9 Hybrid SR cases. This pattern is strongest for the sparse and symbolic methods, which are not simply interpolating the training data. Instead, they are learning compact equations that may generalize well when the test data lie in a less difficult part of the same regime.

The lower test error can also be explained by trajectory difficulty. MSE is sensitive to impacts, contact transitions, large acceleration magnitudes, and high-curvature portions of the motion. If the training subset contains sharper contact events or more extreme acceleration values than the test subset, then the training MSE can be larger even for a valid model. This is especially relevant for the bouncing and flight regimes, where local dynamics can vary strongly across a trajectory.

Report wording:

> The training and testing MSE values are often close because the synthetic train and test segments are generated from the same governing equations within each regime. In some cases, the testing MSE is lower than the training MSE because the model was selected by structural recall rather than by minimum training loss, and because the held-out segment can be dynamically smoother or less difficult than the training segment. Therefore, lower test MSE does not imply that the split is invalid; it indicates that the best-recall model generalizes well to the held-out portion of the same regime.

## Why SR and Hybrid SR Can Have Lower MSE Despite Lower Recall

SINDy and Hybrid SINDy generally perform better in structural recall because their libraries are designed around the expected equation-of-motion terms. Recall measures whether the discovered expression contains the correct analytical terms, so methods with physically targeted libraries are naturally favored when they recover those exact terms. However, MSE measures a different objective: it only evaluates how close the predicted accelerations are to the observed accelerations over the sampled trajectory.

Symbolic Regression and Hybrid SR can therefore achieve lower MSE even when their recall is worse. Symbolic regression searches a broader nonlinear expression space and can find compact surrogate expressions that approximate the acceleration very well over the sampled state range. These expressions may not match the analytical EOM term-by-term, so they receive lower recall, but they can still be accurate predictors on the available trajectory data.

This behavior appears clearly in the best-recall MSE results. Hybrid SR gives the lowest test MSE in 4 of the 9 regime/DOF combinations, while Hybrid SINDy gives the lowest test MSE in 3 of 9. In several of these cases, SR or Hybrid SR has lower recall than SINDy or Hybrid SINDy but still produces lower test MSE. For example, in flight `a_theta`, Hybrid SR has recall 0.50 and test MSE 4.158e-02, while SINDy has recall 1.00 but test MSE 6.999e-01. In rolling `a_x`, SR has recall 0.50 and test MSE 1.325e-04, while SINDy has higher recall 0.60 but test MSE 4.981e-04.

The reason is that recall is an exact structural metric. If SR discovers an expression that is algebraically different from the target EOM terms, or if it absorbs missing physics into a different nonlinear combination, the recall score can be low even if the expression predicts accurately. This is especially likely when the sampled trajectory only covers a limited portion of the state space. Over a limited range of angles, angular velocities, or contact forces, multiple expressions can behave similarly numerically even though only one matches the true physical equation structure.

Hybrid SR can benefit further from regime-specific fitting. Because each regime has more localized behavior, the symbolic search can find a simple local expression that predicts that regime well. This can reduce MSE while still failing to recover all terms of the true global or analytical EOM. Thus, Hybrid SR can be locally predictive but less physically interpretable.

Report wording:

> Although SINDy and Hybrid SINDy often achieve higher structural recall, SR and Hybrid SR can produce lower MSE in some cases because prediction accuracy and equation recovery are different objectives. Recall rewards exact recovery of the analytical EOM terms, while MSE only measures numerical agreement with the observed accelerations. Symbolic regression searches a broader expression space and can discover compact surrogate equations that approximate the sampled trajectory well, even when those equations do not match the true EOM term-by-term. Therefore, SR-based methods can be highly predictive over the observed regime but less reliable as physically interpretable equation-recovery methods.

## Overall Interpretation

The results show a clear separation between structural recovery and predictive accuracy. SINDy and Hybrid SINDy are stronger for recovering recognizable EOM structure, particularly when the correct terms are present in the candidate library. SR and Hybrid SR can sometimes achieve lower MSE because they are more flexible and can approximate the trajectory locally, but this flexibility can reduce physical interpretability and exact term recall.

For this project, the main objective is recovery of the equations of motion. Therefore, recall and coefficient accuracy should remain the primary evidence for physical discovery, while MSE and R2 should be presented as supporting predictive metrics. Lower MSE from SR or Hybrid SR should be interpreted as evidence of local predictive accuracy, not necessarily better recovery of the governing equations.
